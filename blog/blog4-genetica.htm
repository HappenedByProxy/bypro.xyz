/\ByProxy\/ ############################################################## BLOG 4 - Who is Spiral Graphics Inc., anyway? ############################################################## Recently, I decided to look for more information about some software I use, Genetica. Genetica is a procedual, node based seamless texture generator, developed by Spiral Graphics. Don't let the footer fool you, it's just an automatically updated one. The last update to their website was sometime in 2015, where there's been a neverending "holiday discount" since then. If you go to their index, there is a link to their now-dead forums too. By the look of it on archive.org, this was once a pretty popular place. But what happened? I'm trying to find out, primarily from curiousity.

Within the program are some textures that require materials from external servers. Obviously, you can't get these anymore because they're down too. Using Process Monitor, I discovered that it's trying to connect to "apache2-fritz.washington.dreamhost.com". Is it up? Yes! Is it functional? Well, no.

WHOIS

spiralgraphics.biz was registered on 2003/02/06, was last updated on 2018/03/09 and expires on 2027/02/05. It was registered by Alkis Roufas in Colorado. Finally, a name!

SecurityTrails

This website hosts the DNS records and historical data of most websites. Looking at the current records shows the following:

The A Record belongs to New Dream Network
The MX is handled by Amazon
The NS is to dreamhost.com
The SOA is also for dreamhost.com

As for subdomains, there is blog, mail, autoconfig, ftp, webmail and ssh.

The state of Spiral Graphics Inc.

Some searches of this name come up with some pretty consistent results. First of all, we have this profile which matches the company we're looking for!



There was never any official news about Spiral Graphics Inc. closing down. However, their current filing status is "delinquent", meaning they've missed multiple payments. Their last payment was in 2017, given "delinquent" status in 2018 and ever since then there's been silence.




There was one result that I ignored, though. When searching for "Alkis Roufas", there was one result for his "public court records". Of course, you need to pay to see them though. In Colorado, if a business owner dies, the business' estate becomes the new owner of the business. Or, if it's a small business, the business gets terminated. But what if the owner goes to jail?
